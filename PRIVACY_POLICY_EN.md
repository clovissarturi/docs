# Privacy Policy - TennisCountWatch

**Last updated:** February 19, 2026

## 1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how the TennisCountWatch application ("we", "our" or "the App") collects, uses and protects your information when you use our Apple Watch application.

We take your privacy very seriously. TennisCountWatch was developed with a **privacy by design** approach, following Apple's guidelines and data protection best practices.

## 2. Information We DO NOT Collect

TennisCountWatch was designed to function completely offline, without any collection or transmission of personal data to external servers. Specifically:

- ❌ **We do not collect** personally identifiable information
- ❌ **We do not track** your location
- ❌ **We do not access** your contact list
- ❌ **We do not collect** health or fitness data
- ❌ **We do not use** cookies or trackers
- ❌ **We do not send** data to external servers
- ❌ **We do not share** information with third parties
- ❌ **We do not display** ads or analytics
- ❌ **We do not require** account creation or login

## 3. Data Stored Locally on Device

All data generated by using the application is stored **exclusively on your Apple Watch**, through the system's UserDefaults framework. This data includes:

### 3.1 Application Settings
- Player names (user configurable)
- Match preferences (number of sets, tiebreak points, court type)
- Interface settings (alerts, simplified mode)
- Accessibility preferences (voice announcement, Double Tap gesture)
- Preferred application language

### 3.2 Match History
- Final score of completed matches
- Match date and time
- Settings used in each match
- Sets and games statistics

**Important:** All this data remains exclusively on your device and you can delete it at any time through the app settings or by uninstalling the application.

## 4. System Permissions

TennisCountWatch requests the following system permissions, only when necessary and with your explicit consent:

### 4.1 Motion Sensors (CoreMotion)
- **When requested:** Only if you activate the "Double Tap with Wrist Gesture" feature in settings
- **Purpose:** Detect Apple Watch orientation to score points through the Double Tap gesture based on arm position
- **Data collected:** Accelerometer and gyroscope data (pitch, roll, yaw) processed locally
- **Storage:** Data is processed in real-time and **is not stored**. Only the detection result (player 1 or 2) is used momentarily
- **Sharing:** None. Data never leaves the device
- **How to disable:** Disable the "Double Tap" feature in settings or choose "For Server" mode

### 4.2 Voice Synthesis (AVSpeechSynthesizer)
- **When requested:** Only if you activate the "Voice Announcement" feature in settings
- **Purpose:** Announce match score in audio
- **Data collected:** No personal data. The system only uses match scores to generate audio phrases
- **Storage:** None. Audio is synthesized locally and discarded immediately after playback
- **Sharing:** None
- **How to disable:** Disable "Voice Announcement" in settings

### 4.3 Handoff and Continuity
- **Status:** Not used
- TennisCountWatch does not use Handoff or Continuity features between devices

## 5. Data Sharing

TennisCountWatch offers a match results sharing feature through the system's native ShareSheet:

### 5.1 How It Works
- You can share the result of a completed match
- Sharing is done through the standard watchOS/iOS ShareSheet
- Only formatted text with the score is shared
- **You have full control** over when, how and with whom to share

### 5.2 What is Shared
- Player names (as you configured them)
- Final match score
- Result of each set

### 5.3 What is NOT Shared
- No unique device identifier
- No location or time information
- No additional data beyond the score text

## 6. Children's Data

TennisCountWatch does not collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13 years old (or minimum equivalent age in your jurisdiction). Since there is no data collection, the application is safe for use by all ages.

## 7. Data Security

Although we do not collect personal data, we implement security measures to protect locally stored information:

- Use of UserDefaults with iOS/watchOS sandboxing
- Data protected by Apple Watch device encryption
- No data transmission over the internet
- Code developed following Apple's security best practices

## 8. Your Rights and Data Control

You have full control over your data:

### 8.1 Data Access
- You can view your match history at any time through the app's "History" section

### 8.2 Data Editing
- You can change player names and settings at any time

### 8.3 Data Deletion
- **Delete individual matches:** Swipe left on match history
- **Clear all history:** Use the "Clear All" button in the history section
- **Delete all data:** Uninstall the application from your Apple Watch

### 8.4 Data Export
- Use the sharing feature to export match results
